CogCM:
Cognition-Inspired Contextual Modeling for
Audio-Visual Speech Enhancement

Anonymous
Under Review

Abstract

Audio-Visual Speech Enhancement (AVSE) leverages both audio and visual information to improve speech quality. Despite noisy real-world conditions, humans effortlessly perceive and interpret corrupted speech segments. Cognitive science shows how the brain merges auditory and visual inputs to achieve this. From cognitive studies, we derive four key insights, reflecting a hierarchical synergy of visual, semantic, and signal processes: (1) Visual and semantic cues work together to compensate for missing speech signals. (2) Visual input is tightly coupled with semantic context, enabling direct extraction of meaning from visual cues. (3) Visual appearance provides critical information for modeling lower-level signal features. (4) High-level semantic knowledge and low-level auditory processing operate concurrently, allowing semantics to guide signal-level context modeling. Motivated by these insights, we propose CogCM, a cognition-inspired hierarchical contextual modeling framework that leverages high-level semantic information to guide lower-level signal context modeling, thereby recovering corrupted regions more effectively. The CogCM framework includes three core modules: (1) a semantic context modeling module to capture semantic context from audio-visual modalities; (2) a signal context modeling module to model fine-grained temporal-spectral structures under semantic guidance; and (3) a semantic-to-signal guidance module to leverage semantic context in guiding signal context modeling across both temporal and frequency dimensions. Extensive experiments on seven benchmarks demonstrate CogCM's superiority, achieving 63.6% SDR and 58.1% PESQ improvements at -15dB SNR -- outperforming state-of-the-art methods across all metrics.
